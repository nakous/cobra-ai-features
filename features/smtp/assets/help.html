SMTP Documentation

Overview

The SMTP feature allows you to configure custom SMTP and POP settings for WordPress emails. This can significantly improve email deliverability by using a dedicated email service provider instead of relying on your web server's default mail function.

Key Features

  • Configure SMTP settings for reliable email sending
  • Optional POP before SMTP authentication
  • Custom from name and email address
  • Send test emails to verify your configuration
  • Support for TLS and SSL encryption
  • Compatible with major email providers (Gmail, Outlook, SendGrid, etc.)

Settings Explained

General Settings

  • Enable Email Service - Turn the feature on or off.
  • From Email - The email address that will appear in the "From" field of sent emails.
  • From Name - The name that will appear alongside the From Email.

SMTP Settings

  • Enable SMTP - Use SMTP for sending emails instead of PHP's mail() function.
  • SMTP Host - Your email provider's SMTP server address (e.g., smtp.gmail.com).
  • SMTP Port - The port used for SMTP communication (common ports: 25, 465, 587).
  • Encryption - Choose between None, TLS, or SSL based on your provider's requirements.
  • Authentication - Whether your SMTP server requires authentication.
  • Username - Your email account username or full email address.
  • Password - Your email account password or app password.

POP Settings

Some email providers require POP authentication before allowing SMTP sending.

  • Enable POP before SMTP - Use if your provider requires this authentication method.
  • POP Host - Your email provider's POP server address (e.g., pop.gmail.com).
  • POP Port - The port used for POP communication (common ports: 110, 995).
  • Username - Your email account username.
  • Password - Your email account password.

Common Email Provider Settings

Gmail

  • SMTP Host: smtp.gmail.com
  • SMTP Port: 587
  • Encryption: TLS
  • Authentication: Yes
  • Username: Your full Gmail address
  • Password: Your app password (requires 2FA setup)

Outlook/Office 365

  • SMTP Host: smtp.office365.com
  • SMTP Port: 587
  • Encryption: TLS
  • Authentication: Yes
  • Username: Your full Outlook address
  • Password: Your Outlook password or app password

SendGrid

  • SMTP Host: smtp.sendgrid.net
  • SMTP Port: 587
  • Encryption: TLS
  • Authentication: Yes
  • Username: apikey
  • Password: Your SendGrid API key

Troubleshooting

Email Not Sending

  • Verify your SMTP credentials are correct.
  • Check if your host blocks outgoing SMTP connections.
  • Try different port numbers (587 or 465).
  • Check if your email provider requires app passwords.
  • Ensure your From Email is authorized to send through your SMTP provider.

Authentication Errors

  • Confirm your username is in the correct format (sometimes full email is required).
  • Check if you need to create an app-specific password.
  • Verify your account doesn't have security features blocking external access.

Using Gmail

If using Gmail, you need to:

  1. Enable 2-Step Verification in your Google account.
  2. Create an App Password specifically for your WordPress site.
  3. Use this App Password instead of your regular Gmail password.

Test Email

Always use the Test Email functionality to verify your settings. If the test email fails, check the error message for clues about what's wrong with your configuration.

Security Notes

Email credentials are stored in the WordPress database. While we take precautions to store these securely, it's recommended to:

  • Use app-specific passwords when possible instead of your main account password.
  • Create a dedicated email account for sending WordPress emails.
  • Ensure your WordPress site uses HTTPS to encrypt data transmission.
